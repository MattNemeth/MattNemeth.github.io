Projects

Cricket GUI

I have always enjoyed the game of darts. There is something about the game being so simple yet so difficult that I enjoy. My roommates and I had always kept score in our heads since we do not have a chalkboard. I decided I would try and make some sort of GUI to keep track of score more easily. There are many applications and websites that already do this but I wanted to try my hand at making my own. Many other applications often have lots of other features and games that I was not interested in.

I decided to build it using Java with Netbeans IDE. Prior to this project, my only experience with each of these was a simple "Hello World" program. After some research, I concluded that creating a Swing GUI would be the best place to start. I began by sketching a design of how I would like the GUI to look. After playing around in the IDE for a bit, I ended with this design:

While the interface itself isn't exactly eye catching, it works. And that is exactly what my roommates and I needed. Users can interact with the GUI by hitting a "+1" when they score a dart in that section. This makes scoring very simple. After you score, you tap the screen a few times and continue to play. This program allowed us to play more games in less time since we did did not have to stop and make sure the score in our heads was correct.

If you are unfamiliar with the game, Cricket, you can read about how to play here!

Calendar Automator

While watching a youtube video one day, I was heavily inspired by a content creator to simplify my Google Calendar. While the calendar layout is a great visual representation of a schedule, it would be nice to have that schedule in hard numbers. Something to take a schedule in Google Calendar and translate that to how many hours is planned to be spent and on what. So that is what was set out to be created.

Using Google Calendar's API, I successfully connected my google account and was able to the use a script to grab the events from my google calendar and translate them directly into hours. Experience

L3 Technologies - Space and Sensors Sector, Engineering Intern

Overview

I have spent my first three co-op semesters at L3 Techonologies located in Mason, Ohio. Each semester I was a part of their Digital Design group working on the digital logic logic inside of their FPGA's for their cameras.

Responsibilites

  • Submit Change notices and ensure that all relevant documentation is up to date

  • Develop reusable, generic VHDL synchronizers

  • Design schematics from ports list

  • Collaborated with other engineers to ensure obsolete parts on our PCBs had working replacements

What I Learned

  • The Devil is in the Details!

    During my third semester I was given more difficult taks. I quickly realized the importance of being attentitive to details. The smallest differences can make the biggest changes and it is important as an engineer to notice them.

  • Create a Plan!

    Creating a plan instead of jumping right in can save enormous amounts of time. What I found to be most effective was to draw out my ideas before I get started. Once I felt I thought through exactly what needed to be done, I confirmed my understanding and plan with other engineers. It can be difficult but it is important to resist the urge to jump right in.

  • Books are important!

    While the internet is extremely useful, books are a very valuable resource that should not be forgotten!

  • Digital Design is not for me!

    While I enjoyed the opportunity to work with highly intelligent individuals and learn a lot about how to design high level circuits, I do not have the passion for the work. Almost every small project I have worked on at home has been software related. Sometimes it is more helpful to learn what you don't like than what you love.
